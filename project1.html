REACH Senior Design

Description

At REACH (Rehabilitative EMG-Assisted Control for Health), we are leveraging electromyography (EMG) and functional electrical stimulation (FES) to aid stroke patients experiencing upper limb impairments.

Our team collaborated with the ICS department to develop our ML model to categorize hand gestures through EMG signal analysis.

As the design lead, I created and prototyped the initial CAD design of our device using SolidWorks. Recognizing our team’s limited experience in developing custom functional electrical stimulation units, we incorporated a commercially available FES device, to deliver custom stimulation based on patient needs.

I helped integrate the FES unit by engineering a control system using two stepper motors, allowing precise adjustment of stimulation intensity across two channels. Additionally, I integrated four relays to control the frequency, wavelength, and stimulation durations through a Raspberry Pi 5.
